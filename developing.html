Choropleth map - Life Expectancy

In this first graph it is possible to visualize the entire maps of the world divided per country.
In the choropleth map we want to display the worldwide situation of life expectancy value in all the "developing" countries during the year 2015.
Thanks to a blue color map (light blue to dark blue) we can understand in which country we have the highest value of life expectancy, the lightest color of blue is used for labeled the "Developed" countries.
Trough the tooltip we can clearly visualize all the life expectancy countries situation.
We can see that the greatest amount of developing countries are centered in Africa (in this case we can clearly see , especially in center Africa , that le life expectancy is low) , South America and Asia.

Grouped barplot of immunization coverage per most populated states

In this plot we want to display the immunization coverage for ten of the most populated "Developing" countries , using a grouped barplot
For this experiment , the Kaggle dataset gave us the data for the immunzation coverage of 3 different diseases : HepatisB (Red) , Polio (Blue) and Diphteria (Green).
In the Y axis there is the percentage of immunization among 1 year olds and in the x axis the countries.
The 3 different colors bars represent the 3 different type of disease.
Having a good coverage immunization for the majority of the diseases it is very important for increasing the life expectancy of a country and for aspiring to become a "Developed" state .
We can see that few countries have a high percentage of immunization for these disease.
Under this point of view only Turkey is doing a good job.
You can use the tooltip for visualizing the amount of population , the precise percentage of immunization

Sankey plot of deaths divided per ages

In the first part of our analysis of the "Developing" countries , we are going to visualize a Sankey Diagram.
In the first block we have the total amount of population of the top five "Developing" countries considering the year 2015.
In the middle of the graph there all the the countries we are taking into account . Bigger block means bigger amount of population.
The last part of the visualzion is the most important one.
We have three different blocks : Adult Deaths , Infant Deaths and Others.
Adult Deaths is the probability of dying between 15 and 60 years , Infants Deaths is the number of infant people , Others are other ages intervals probably of elderly people and teenagers (it would have been more interesting if the dataset provided more columns)
We can see , for example , that in Nigeria the number of Infant deaths is greater than the Adult deaths becuase the life condition are very difficult to handle when you are youger.
Tooltip is provided for see clearly all the values. It is useful to say some words about it. If the life expectancy of a country is quite high , so the number of Infant Deaths should be low. This happens , for example , with Turkey which has a not so low life expectancy and the number of Infant Deaths is very low.

Timeline from 2000 to 2015

In the last part of this analysis , we would like to check the trend of life expectancy during the years starting from 2000 to 2015.
With this kind of visualization , we have the possibility to analyze precisely the trend of each country thanks to the dropdown menu and thanks to the tooltip that will display the life expectancy at the selected year.
It could be useful analyze some of these countries.
The first to analyze deeper is Haiti. This island has been a quite stable trend for all the years considered but with an exception. During the 2010 , the inhabitants of Haiti have been hit by a terrible earthquake , for this reason the life expectancy value has dropped drammatically in that year (click here, if you want more information about this event).
Another country to analyze could be Rwanda. Rwanda is a country located in Africa and in 2000 its life expectancy was 48.3 , not a a so high value. But, in the years after , life expectancy has been increased noticeably , reaching 68.0 years old. An article in the website of United Nations explain this situation and what is going on in Africa (you can find it here).
